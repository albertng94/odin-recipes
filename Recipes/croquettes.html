Jam Croquettes

Everyone's favourite tapa

Croquettes are probably a universally liked tapa which is tradition in Spain. They can come in many flavours, but I have chosen one of the most typical and tasty, the jam-filled croquette.

Ingredients (for 3 people)

For the croquettes dough:

  • 100g of cured jam
  • Half an onion
  • A clove of garlic
  • 50g of flour
  • 50g of butter (or 50ml of extra virgin olive oil)
  • 0.5l of milk
  • Parsley

For the coat and frying:

  • Flour
  • 2 beat eggs
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Preparation

  1. Cut the butter into dices and add it to a pot for to slowly melt. Cut the onion and the garlic small and add them to the pot with the butter. Cook the mix for 5 to 6 minutes, and add the jam in small cuts. Stir-fry the mix.
  2. Pour the flour into the pot and stir fry again. When the flour has some color, start adding milk little by little while constantly stiring the mix. Cook the mix (bechamel) for about 20 minutes with a medium heat while constantly stiring. Add some small-cut parsley.
  3. Pour the croquettes mass onto a platter and cover it with some fil paper so it doesn't dry. Let it cool.
  4. When the mass has cooled, cut it and mold the croquettes.
  5. Coat the croquettes in flour firstly, the beat egg afterwards, and fry them in hot olive oil.
  6. When the croquettes are fried, take them out of the oil and dry them from the excess of oil by leaving them on a kitchen paper covered dish.
  7. Serve and enjoy them while still hot :P.
